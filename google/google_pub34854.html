   Estimating the Spectral Reflectance of Natural Imagery Using Color Image Features

Abstract: Relative spectral reﬂectance is an illumination invariant image feature that is related to many ecological phenomena that are difﬁcult to measure, such as plant CO2 uptake. We describe a procedure to estimate the relative spectral reﬂectance of known subject using color image features. Through application, we show that this procedure produces accurate estimates in the presence of changing ﬁeld conditions. Using this procedure, we can use imagers as sensors to measure natural phenomena that cannot be easily measured using any other available sensing modality.
