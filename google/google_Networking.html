Networking

What We Do

Internet research: Today's broadband networks are not like traditional networks such as railroads, or even circuit-switched telephone networks. Their vast matrix of interdependencies and shifting conditions make them fundamentally different, and require fundamentally different and novel approaches to understand them. National Regulatory Agencies and those tasked with understanding broadband network performance must recognize these differences, and match the novelty of the task they are now undertaking with new, scientific approaches to gathering information. Google’s research efforts in this area focus on creating a platform for Internet-scale measurement, driven by academic researchers, which produces data that can be analyzed for the purpose of providing stable platforms for sound public policy.

SDN research: Google has been a leader in Software Defined Networking, helping drive innovation through novel approaches to building and operating networks. Google’s research efforts in this space combine developing standards that provide a foundation for a broad range of solutions to be explored with producing examples of specific solutions that illustrate the practical value of the SDN approach to networking.
