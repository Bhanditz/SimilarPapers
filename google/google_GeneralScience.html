General Science

What We Do

Most of the research done at Google is in various areas of computer science, but computer science now impacts all aspects of science, in much the same way that mathematics has traditionally been used to describe laws of nature.  This is particularly true in the era of "big data", which enables new approaches to scientific investigation.  The traditional cycle of scientific investigation consists of observation, modeling, hypothesis, and testing.  Unfortunately, the mathematical models that are constructed this way are often simplistic and fail to adequately capture the underlying complexity that exists in the world. The advent of "big data" allows us to represent models explicitly through the data itself, and form hypotheses directly from the observed data.  This trend shows up in the fields of genomics, medicine, astronomy, electrical engineering, mathematics, statistics, sociology, and other areas.
